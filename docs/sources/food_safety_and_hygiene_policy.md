# Food Safety and Hygiene Policy

**Version:** 2025.1
**Scope:** All partner restaurants and delivery drivers operating on the delivery service platform.
**Purpose:** Define the minimum food safety and hygiene standards that partner kitchens and drivers must follow to protect customer health, meet regulatory obligations, and uphold the delivery service's quality commitments.

This policy is informed by the FDA Food Code (2022) and EU Regulation 852/2004 on the hygiene of foodstuffs. Partner restaurants remain responsible for meeting all local regulatory requirements in their jurisdiction; this document sets the operational floor.

---

## §1. Temperature control

Prepared food must be held, transported, and delivered within strict temperature bands to prevent bacterial growth in the "danger zone" between 5°C and 60°C.

### Hot food

- Hot-held food must be kept at **or above 60°C** (140°F) from preparation until handover to the driver.
- During transit, hot food must be packaged in insulated delivery bags that keep the internal temperature **above 57°C (135°F)** for at least 45 minutes.
- If a hot meal measures below 57°C at the handover point, the driver must decline the order and notify operations immediately — see Delivery Operations Handbook §4.

### Cold food

- Cold-held food (sushi, salads, desserts, dairy products) must be kept at **or below 5°C** (41°F) from preparation until handover.
- During transit, cold food must be packaged in chilled insulated bags that keep the internal temperature **below 8°C (46°F)** for at least 45 minutes.
- Raw fish (sashimi, nigiri) has a tighter bound: it must stay below 5°C throughout the entire journey.

### Temperature logs

Partner restaurants must log the core temperature of at least one prepared meal per hour during operating hours and retain the log for a minimum of 90 days. Operations may request logs during audits or customer complaint investigations.

## §2. Personal hygiene

All kitchen staff and drivers handling food must follow these standards:

- **Handwashing:** Wash hands with soap and warm water for at least 20 seconds before handling food, after handling raw meat, after using the restroom, after touching the face or hair, and between tasks.
- **Gloves:** Disposable food-safe gloves must be worn when handling ready-to-eat food. Gloves must be changed at least every 30 minutes or between tasks.
- **Uniforms:** Clean aprons or uniforms must be worn during shifts. No open-toed shoes in the kitchen.
- **Hair restraint:** Long hair must be tied back; hair nets or caps are recommended in prep areas.
- **Illness reporting:** Staff exhibiting symptoms of gastroenteritis, vomiting, diarrhea, jaundice, or open wounds on hands must be excluded from food handling until cleared to return. Managers must report exclusions to operations within 24 hours.

---

## §3. Cross-contamination prevention

Cross-contamination is the most common cause of foodborne illness and of allergen-related incidents. Every partner kitchen must implement the following controls:

- **Separate preparation zones:** Raw meat, raw fish, and ready-to-eat foods must be prepared on separate surfaces and with separate utensils. Color-coded cutting boards (red for raw meat, blue for raw fish, green for produce, white for cooked/ready-to-eat) are strongly encouraged.
- **Allergen segregation:** Dishes marked as containing a regulated allergen (see Allergen Labeling Policy) must be prepared away from allergen-free dishes. A separate wash cycle is required for utensils used on nut-based dishes before they touch a nut-free order.
- **Gluten-free claims:** A dish may be labeled "gluten-free" only if prepared in a dedicated gluten-free zone with dedicated utensils and cookware. If the kitchen cannot guarantee isolation (e.g., the Margherita Pizza gluten-free base option), the customer must be informed that cross-contamination is possible — see Customer Service Playbook §2 for the script.
- **Raw ingredient storage:** Raw meat and raw fish must be stored in sealed containers on the bottom shelf of the refrigerator, below any ready-to-eat items. Vegetables and cooked foods go above.
- **No double-dipping:** Spoons, ladles, and tasting utensils must never be reused between dishes without washing.

---

## §4. Packaging

- **Tamper-evident seals:** Every delivery must be sealed with a tamper-evident sticker or sealed container before handover to the driver. If a seal is broken on arrival, the customer is entitled to a full refund — see Refund and SLA Policy §2.
- **Insulated bags:** Drivers must use the delivery service's branded insulated delivery bags. Personal bags are not permitted.
- **Separation of hot and cold:** Hot and cold items in the same order must be placed in separate insulated compartments. Mixing is not permitted.
- **No re-use:** Single-use packaging may not be reused between orders. Drivers must never transfer food between containers during transit.

---

## §5. Cleaning and sanitation

- Kitchen surfaces must be cleaned with food-grade sanitizer at least every 4 hours during service.
- Floors must be mopped and sanitized at the end of each service shift.
- Refrigerator thermometers must be checked daily; readings outside the required band (5°C for cold hold) trigger an immediate equipment review.
- Garbage must be removed from the preparation area at least every 2 hours during peak service and stored in sealed bins away from food.

---

## §6. Incident reporting

Any suspected foodborne illness, contamination event, or customer complaint mentioning illness symptoms must be reported to operations **within 2 hours** of the incident. The operations team will open a Food Safety Incident Report (FSIR) and, if the complaint is linked to a specific partner kitchen, initiate a 24-hour audit.

See Customer Service Playbook §5 for the customer-side handling flow.

---

## §7. Audits and compliance

- Partner restaurants are audited by the delivery service at least once per quarter.
- An audit failure on a temperature band, cross-contamination control, or personal hygiene standard results in a **30-day corrective action plan**. Repeat failures trigger contract review.
- Drivers are spot-audited on bag cleanliness and temperature compliance at random handover points.

Questions about this policy should be directed to `foodsafety@deliveryservice.internal`. Partner restaurants can request a refresher training session for staff at no cost once per year.
