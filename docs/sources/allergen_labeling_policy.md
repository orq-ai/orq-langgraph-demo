# Allergen Labeling Policy

**Version:** 2025.1
**Scope:** All dishes listed in the Menu Book and displayed in the delivery service's customer-facing app.
**Purpose:** Ensure every dish sold through the platform carries accurate, consistent allergen information so customers with food allergies can make informed choices.

This policy is informed by EU Regulation 1169/2011 on the provision of food information to consumers. Partner restaurants outside the EU must still meet this standard as a contractual obligation.

---

## §1. The 14 regulated allergens

The following 14 allergens must be explicitly listed on every dish if present, regardless of quantity:

1. **Gluten** — wheat, rye, barley, oats, spelt, kamut, and their hybridised strains
2. **Crustaceans** — prawns, lobster, crab, crayfish, and products thereof
3. **Eggs** — hen eggs and products thereof
4. **Fish** — all finfish species and products thereof (including anchovy paste in Caesar dressing)
5. **Peanuts** — peanuts and products thereof
6. **Soy** — soybeans and products thereof (including soy sauce, miso, tofu)
7. **Dairy** — milk and products thereof, including lactose
8. **Nuts** — almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, Brazil nuts, pistachios, macadamia, Queensland nuts
9. **Celery** — celery and products thereof
10. **Mustard** — mustard and products thereof
11. **Sesame** — sesame seeds and products thereof (including tahini, sesame buns)
12. **Sulphites** — sulphur dioxide and sulphites at concentrations above 10 mg/kg
13. **Lupin** — lupin and products thereof
14. **Molluscs** — mussels, oysters, squid, octopus, and products thereof

The Menu Book uses shorter common names (e.g., "nuts" instead of "tree nuts", "dairy" instead of "milk") that map to the above categories. Customer support agents should read the allergen list on each dish strictly — if a dish lists "nuts", assume it may contain any of the 8 tree nut types above.

---

## §2. Labeling requirements

### On-menu display

Every dish in the Menu Book must include an `Allergens:` line. When no regulated allergen is present, the line must read `Allergens: None`. **An empty or missing allergen line is not acceptable** — it must explicitly state "None".

### On-app display

The customer-facing app shows allergen information as a colored-icon row under each dish. The 14 allergens above each have an icon. Icons must match the dish's `Allergens:` line from the Menu Book. Any mismatch is a P1 incident — see Customer Service Playbook §5.

### Traces and cross-contamination

If a dish *may contain* an allergen due to shared kitchen equipment — but does not intentionally contain it — the menu entry must include a `May contain:` line in addition to the `Allergens:` line. Example:

> **Allergens:** gluten, dairy
> **May contain:** nuts (prepared in a kitchen that handles nuts)

Partner restaurants are responsible for declaring cross-contamination risks honestly. A restaurant that serves nut-based dishes must label all its non-nut dishes with `May contain: nuts` unless it operates a strictly segregated nut-free zone (see Food Safety and Hygiene Policy §3).

## §3. Special cases

### Sulphites in wine and dried fruit

Sulphites are commonly present in wine, dried fruits, and some vinegars. If a dish uses an ingredient with >10 mg/kg sulphites, it must be declared even if the primary listing does not otherwise require allergen labeling. The Tiramisu (which contains Marsala wine) is an example — its allergen list includes sulphites where applicable.

### Fish in savory sauces

Some sauces and dressings contain hidden fish allergens — notably Caesar dressing (anchovies), Thai fish sauce, and Worcestershire sauce. Dishes using these sauces must declare `fish` on the allergen line. Example: the Caesar Salad declares `fish` for this reason.

### Soy in East Asian cuisine

Soy sauce, miso, tamari, and tofu all count as soy. Dishes using any of these must declare `soy`. The Miso Soup, Salmon Nigiri, Tuna Sashimi, and Ramen Shoyu all declare `soy` for this reason.

### Nut-containing curries and sauces

Cashews and almonds are commonly ground into Indian curry sauces as a thickener. Dishes using nut-based sauces must declare `nuts`. The Chicken Tikka Masala, Paneer Butter Masala, and Lamb Biryani all declare `nuts` for this reason.

## §4. Customer allergy enquiries

When a customer contacts support with an allergy question, the agent must:

1. Refer the customer to the dish's on-app or Menu Book allergen list.
2. **Never** advise the customer that a dish is "safe" for their allergy — only state what is listed.
3. Remind the customer that cross-contamination is possible in shared kitchens unless the menu explicitly states the dish is prepared in a segregated allergen-free zone.
4. If the customer reports a reaction after eating, follow the Food Safety Incident Report flow in Customer Service Playbook §5.

---

## §5. Menu updates

When a partner restaurant changes a recipe, the allergen list must be updated in the Menu Book **within 24 hours** of the recipe change. Until the update is live, the restaurant must pause the affected dish on the app to prevent mislabeled orders.

Questions about this policy should be directed to `compliance@deliveryservice.internal`.
